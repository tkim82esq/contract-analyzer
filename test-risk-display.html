Extended Payment Terms

Payment Terms

HIGH

The payment terms require NET 90 payment, meaning you won't receive payment for three months after invoicing. For a Service Provider, this creates severe cash flow pressure, forcing you to fund three months of operational expenses before seeing revenue. Industry standard is NET 30, so this triples your working capital requirements. If you have multiple clients on similar terms, you could face bankruptcy despite profitable contracts. Small agencies have failed simply from cash flow timing when clients delay payments beyond even these extended terms.

Recommendation:

Negotiate to change payment terms to NET 30 or shorter. Request milestone-based payments to improve cash flow. Consider requiring partial upfront payment or requesting a retainer to reduce cash flow risk.

Ambiguous Payment Terms

Payment Terms

MEDIUM

The contract states payment is due "upon satisfactory completion" without defining what constitutes satisfactory work or who determines acceptance. This vague language gives the client unlimited discretion to delay or refuse payment based on subjective criteria. In consulting projects, such ambiguous payment terms have led to disputes where clients withhold payment for months, claiming work doesn't meet undefined standards. For a service provider operating on tight cash flow, this could mean waiting 6+ months for payment on completed work, potentially forcing you to pursue expensive legal action to collect what you're owed.

Recommendation:

Request specific, measurable acceptance criteria for each deliverable and add a provision that work is deemed accepted if not disputed within 5 business days with specific written objections.
